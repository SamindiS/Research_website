AI-Based Multi-Sensory Behavioral Screening for Early Autism Detection

SenseAI is an AI-powered multi-sensory behavioral screening system designed to support early detection of Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). It integrates gaze tracking, behavioral analysis, response-to-name testing, and cognitive flexibility assessment to provide objective and scalable screening.

01 · Literature Survey

What prior research tells us

Existing ASD screening methods rely heavily on subjective observation and questionnaires, which can delay diagnosis and introduce inconsistencies across different clinical environments. Recent computer-vision and ML studies show measurable behavioral biomarkers — gaze patterns, response latency, and motor responses — but few production-ready, multi-sensory systems exist.

  • Manual observation tools remain the clinical default (M-CHAT, ADOS).
  • Eye-tracking and head-pose studies show high diagnostic value.
  • ML pipelines for behavioral data remain mostly research-only.
02 · Research Gap

The blind spot we are addressing

There is a critical lack of scalable, objective, and technology-driven screening systems that can be seamlessly integrated into real-world clinical and home environments. Current solutions are either expensive specialist tools or oversimplified questionnaires — nothing bridges the gap.

  • No affordable multi-sensory screening tool for early-stage ASD.
  • Limited explainability in existing ML-based attempts.
  • Few systems combine gaze + behavior + auditory + cognitive signals.
03 · Research Problem

Why early detection is hard today

Early ASD detection remains difficult due to limited access to specialized pediatricians, high costs, and a heavy reliance on manual, time-consuming assessment techniques. Families in remote regions can wait months or years for a clinical diagnosis.

  • Long waiting lists for specialist appointments.
  • High per-assessment cost limits accessibility.
  • Subjective scoring varies between clinicians.
04 · Research Objectives

What SenseAI sets out to deliver

  • Develop an AI-based multi-sensory screening system.
  • Capture behavioral features objectively from interactive tasks.
  • Provide fast, reliable, and explainable predictions.
  • Support clinicians with decision-ready insights and reports.
  • Run on a child-friendly mobile interface for home or clinic.
05 · Methodology

From interaction to insight

Data is collected through interactive child-friendly tasks via a mobile interface, processed using feature extraction pipelines, and analyzed with machine learning models (Logistic Regression, Random Forest) to generate a comprehensive screening report.
